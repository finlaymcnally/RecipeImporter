## Top spend sinks

1. **Non-recipe knowledge review is the dominant sink by a wide margin.** `overview.md` gives the topline: 25,274,159 knowledge tokens out of 34,178,945 total tokens, a 73.95% share. `codexfarm/prompt_budget_summary.json` shows that the stage ran 5 calls over 5 shards, took 372,645 ms, and burned 12,984,233 wrapper-overhead tokens by itself. It also reports `missing_output_shard_count=5`, `validated_shard_count=0`, and `scored_effect="seed_only"`, while `analysis.knowledge` reports `outputs_parsed_total=0` and `snippets_written_total=0`. This is the sharpest spend target in the packet. 

2. **Recipe correction is the second major sink and the evidence of useful work is weak.** `overview.md` anchors this stage at 7,122,882 tokens. `codexfarm/prompt_budget_summary.json` shows 29 calls, 3,701,342 wrapper-overhead tokens, and 184 command executions. `_upload_bundle_derived/starter_pack_v1/04_warning_and_trace_summary.json` says `correction_empty_mapping_count=29` and `correction_empty_mapping_with_nonempty_output_count=29`. In `analysis.stage_separated_comparison`, all 29 recipes have `mapping_count=0`; 12 have `ingredient_count=0` and `step_count=0` and are later `build_final_recipe=skipped`, while the other 17 end with final `mapping_status="unclear"`. That is a lot of spend for outputs that never produce usable mapping/link structure in this packet. 

3. **Line-role is smaller but still materially expensive relative to observed benefit.** `overview.md` anchors line-role at 1,781,904 tokens. `codexfarm/prompt_budget_summary.json` shows 985,691 wrapper-overhead tokens, 42 command executions, and 5 shard calls. `_upload_bundle_derived/root/comparison_summary.json` shows zero changed lines and zero score delta versus vanilla even though codex had line-role enabled and vanilla did not. So this stage is not the first cut, but the current contract is still over-spending for what this packet actually got back. 

4. **Review-packet waste exists, but it is secondary here.** The packet’s own Oracle turn-1 estimate is about 443,667 tokens, but the bundle is already curated: the default packet keeps `prompt_budget_summary`, prompt samples, call inventory, and warning/trace summary, while heavy artifacts such as the full prompt log and prompt-side knowledge context are omitted or deprioritized. That means the big runtime cuts are still much larger than the packet-format cuts in this specific bundle. 

## Likely waste buckets

1. **Wrapper and worker-contract overhead is the biggest cross-stage waste bucket.** `codexfarm/prompt_budget_summary.json` reports 17,671,266 total wrapper-overhead tokens across the run, versus only 16,888 visible input tokens and 2,727 visible output tokens. All three active stages carry the `command_execution_detected` pathological flag, and the run totals show 451 command executions consuming 28,288,960 command-execution tokens. The packet is pointing at shell/file-readback churn, repeated worker instructions, and session telemetry as the primary waste mechanism—not model reasoning depth. 

2. **The knowledge loop looks like pure burn in this packet.** The call inventory shows all 5 `nonrecipe_knowledge_review` calls returning `payload: null` with `validation_errors: ["missing_output"]`. That matches the prompt-budget summary’s `missing_output_shard_count=5` and the knowledge summary’s `outputs_parsed_total=0`. Since the pair comparison is still exactly flat versus vanilla, this looks like spend with no measurable benchmark return here. 

3. **Fragmentary recipes are being sent through an LLM path that later skips them anyway.** In `analysis.stage_separated_comparison`, 12 of 29 recipe entries have `ingredient_count=0`, `step_count=0`, `mapping_count=0`, and then `build_final_recipe.status="skipped"`. That is a strong sign that a deterministic fragmentary gate could have short-circuited part of the recipe-correction path without changing observed outcomes in this packet. 

4. **Mapping/link instructions are likely readback waste right now.** Every recipe-correction shard in call inventory has `mapping_count=0`, and the stage summary says all 29 correction outputs had empty mappings. The final-stage summary is only `missing` or `unclear`; there is no evidence in this packet that always-on mapping/link work is paying for itself. 

5. **Packet-local upper bound is very large, but it is not the safest default.** On this one pair, codex and vanilla are identical on changed lines, overall accuracy, and macro F1, so the empirical upper bound is that all codex-backed spend was unnecessary for this specific book/scope. I would not promote “disable everything” as a general default from one pair, because `pair_count_sufficient_for_generalization` is false, but it does tell you that aggressive cuts are plausible here if they are gated narrowly. 

## Lowest-risk cuts

1. **Gate off non-recipe knowledge review whenever the stage is not producing parsed outputs and benchmark scoring is not consuming anything beyond the seed.** For this packet, that is the clearest low-risk cut: the stage is `seed_only`, all 5 shards are missing-output, parsed outputs are zero, snippets written are zero, and the codex-vs-vanilla delta is exactly zero. Packet-local save: about **25.27M tokens**. I would roll this out as a narrow gate, not a global policy, because this bundle has only one comparable pair. 

2. **If knowledge must stay, keep the model but replace the live workspace contract with direct JSON-in/JSON-out.** The data does not say the stage is overpowered; it says the contract is bloated. Preserve the same schema, same checker, and same shard boundaries, but stop making the worker read repo files, manage phases, and run repeated helper commands. That targets the **12.98M knowledge wrapper tokens** and the heavy command-execution churn with much lower quality risk than a model downgrade. 

3. **Add a deterministic fragmentary short-circuit before recipe correction.** If `build_intermediate_det` yields `ingredient_count=0` and `step_count=0`, stamp `fragmentary` or `not_a_recipe` immediately and skip correction/final assembly for that recipe. This packet shows 12 recipes in exactly that state, all of which later ended in `build_final_recipe=skipped` anyway. At current average recipe-correction cost, that is roughly **2.95M tokens** of likely removable spend. 

4. **Split recipe correction into a default extraction pass and an opt-in mapping/link pass.** Right now the main recipe-correction contract appears to pay for mapping/link scaffolding on every recipe even though all observed `mapping_count` values are zero and final mapping is only `missing` or `unclear`. Safer path: make mapping optional by default, and only escalate to a linker when a deterministic pre-linker finds at least one plausible anchor. That preserves current outputs better than deleting recipe correction wholesale, but still attacks the waste the packet is actually showing. 

5. **Flatten line-role into a single structured classification call with validator-backed postcheck.** Again, the evidence points to contract waste more than model waste. Keep the same label policy and even the same model if you want; just remove the bounded-workspace phase loop, file-open choreography, and helper-command round trips. That should recover much of the **985k line-role wrapper tokens**. A stronger packet-local move is to disable line-role entirely for this scope/book, but I would keep that as a narrow experiment rather than the default recommendation from one pair. 

6. **Do not expand the token-lane review packet by default.** This packet is already near the right shape for token review: `overview.md`, `index.json`, `prompt_budget_summary`, prompt samples, call inventory, and warning/trace summary were enough to rank cuts. Keep raw request/response logs, full prompt logs, and raw wrong-line context as explicit follow-up-only artifacts. That is a smaller save than runtime changes, but it is the right stance for review-packet discipline.
