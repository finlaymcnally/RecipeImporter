# Salt Fat Knowledge Over-Prune Analysis
**Date:** 2026-04-15_20.58.58
**Author:** Codex
**Subject:** Compare `2026-04-15_12.45.29` vs `2026-04-15_13.39.55` for `saltfatacidheatcutdown` knowledge rows after the stricter line-role exclude change

## 1. Executive Summary

This report compares the two `saltfatacidheatcutdown` benchmark runs requested in `recipeimport-921`:

- `2026-04-15_12.45.29`
- `2026-04-15_13.39.55`

The key result is that the stricter `nonrecipe_excluded` path is only a small part of the knowledge regression.

- Gold `KNOWLEDGE` rows: `679` in both runs
- Old run wrong on gold `KNOWLEDGE`: `39`
- New run wrong on gold `KNOWLEDGE`: `51`
- Net regression: `+12`
- Newly wrong gold `KNOWLEDGE` rows: `23`
- Recovered gold `KNOWLEDGE` rows: `11`
- Newly wrong rows caused by new `nonrecipe_excluded`: `4`
- Newly excluded gold `KNOWLEDGE` rows total: `5`, but one of them was already wrong before the exclude change

Conclusion:

- There is a real over-prune bug in the stricter exclude path.
- That bug is narrow and high-precision: only `5` of `249` newly excluded rows have gold `KNOWLEDGE`.
- Most of the new knowledge misses (`19` of `23`) are not exclude-driven. They are ordinary semantic drift from `KNOWLEDGE -> OTHER` with no `nonrecipe_excluded` reason attached.

## 2. Method

I compared `line-role-pipeline/joined_line_table.jsonl` for both runs by stable `sample_id`.

For each canonical row I tracked:

- `gold_label`
- `pred_label`
- `escalation_reasons`
- `within_recipe_span`

I then isolated rows where:

- `gold_label == "KNOWLEDGE"`
- the old and new run differed

This lets us distinguish:

- genuine regressions caused by the stricter exclude gate
- unrelated semantic relabeling drift
- recoveries that offset part of the regression

## 4. Newly Excluded Gold Knowledge Rows

These are the five gold `KNOWLEDGE` rows that became newly `nonrecipe_excluded` in the later run.

### 4.1 Clearly over-pruned

1. `Set the average home oven to 350°F, and it'll heat up to about 370°F before the heating element shuts off.`
2. `Depending on how sensitive the thermostat is, the temperature might drop down to 330°F before the heating element switches on again.`

Assessment:

- These are straightforward factual cooking-knowledge statements.
- They are not fluff, anecdote, or discourse framing.
- Hard-excluding them is a real routing error.

### 4.2 Probably over-pruned, but more borderline

1. `Before setting out to cook-as you choose an ingredient, or a cooking method-ask yourself, "How can I season this from within?"`
2. `I love to slow-roast salmon, a method that works equally well for a single serving or a whole side.`

Assessment:

- These lines mix discourse voice with genuine technique guidance.
- They are exactly the kind of row that should stay in candidate space and let the semantic stage decide.
- Hard-excluding them is too strong.

### 4.3 Questionable gold, not a real regression

1. `Have you ever added oil into a hot pan for sautéing vegetables and then been distracted by a ringing phone?`

Assessment:

- This row was already wrong in the old run.
- The surrounding passage is a rhetorical setup for the actual smoke-point explanation that follows.
- Gold `KNOWLEDGE` here looks weak. I would not treat this row as evidence that the new exclude rule is broken.

## 5. The Bigger Regression Is Not Exclude-Driven

The later run also introduced `19` new `KNOWLEDGE -> OTHER` misses with no `nonrecipe_excluded` reason.

Representative examples:

- `All salt comes from the ocean...`
- `Fat enhances flavor in another extraordinary way.`
- `The fast method? Browning foods.`
- `Working with hot sugar is one of the few temperature-specific endeavors in the kitchen...`
- `Take, for example, the chicken you're thinking about roasting for dinner.`

These rows are not being dropped by the stricter exclude gate. They are ordinary semantic relabeling drift, so they need a separate investigation.

## 6. Recommendation

### 6.1 Follow-up fix

Treat `nonrecipe_excluded` as a narrow hard-stop only for rows that are clearly discourse-only.

Do not hard-exclude outside-recipe rows that still carry cooking knowledge such as:

- factual temperature/process claims
- generalized method advice
- mixed lead-in lines that introduce concrete technique content

For those rows, the safer contract is:

- keep them in nonrecipe candidate space
- let the semantic stage decide `KNOWLEDGE` vs `OTHER`

This stays aligned with the repo rule that deterministic code should package evidence and preserve authority boundaries, not make brittle semantic calls.

### 6.2 Separate follow-up

Track the `19` non-exclude `KNOWLEDGE -> OTHER` regressions separately.

They are the majority of the observed damage in this run pair and should not be conflated with the stricter exclude change.
