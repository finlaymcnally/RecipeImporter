## 3) Definitions (for no-context readers)

- Config: one importer/parser/deterministic-option variant in all-method.
- Prediction phase: conversion/split/extraction/parsing/writing work that produces predictions for scoring.
- Evaluation phase: canonical-text scoring step (`evaluate_canonical_text`) that compares predictions against gold.
- Split slots / heavy slots: scheduler-limited capacity for expensive split/convert work; when full, configs wait.
- Eval signature reuse: identical prediction outputs share one evaluation result, so not every config reruns eval.

## 8) What This Means For Next Optimization Pass

If the objective is faster all-method wall time for this workload, prioritize:

- reducing split wait (`split_wait_seconds`) and split convert duration (`split_convert_seconds`),
- improving prediction-phase throughput and scheduling fairness under heavy-slot saturation,
- increasing eval-signature reuse opportunities only if it does not distort experiment intent.

Do not prioritize further matcher-engine swaps unless new evidence shows eval wall rising again.

## 9) Sanity Check Targets for Future Runs

Track these as first-class regressions:

- Keep `sum all_method_eval_wall_seconds / sum all_method_prediction_wall_seconds` near current low level.
- Reduce `sum split_wait_seconds` materially.
- Reduce `sum split_convert_seconds` without changing scoring behavior.
- Watch heavy-slot utilization + idle gap together (high utilization is good, but high wait indicates queue pressure).
