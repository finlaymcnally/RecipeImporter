# Fix Outside-Recipe Chapter Taxonomy Overstructuring

## Problem

The refactored line-role policy was treating outside-recipe chapter headings such as `The Four Elements of Good Cooking`, `SALT`, and `What is Salt?` as recipe-local structure.
That inflated pseudo-recipe spans early, starved knowledge review later, and hurt the single-book canonical-text benchmark.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Atomized outside-recipe chapter-taxonomy heading runs stay `OTHER` unless they have real nearby recipe scaffold.
- Bare outside-recipe ingredient-like tokens no longer support recipe-title promotion by themselves.
- A focused regression covers the Salt Fat Acid Heat heading trio with real atomizer rule tags.

## Verification

- `. .venv/bin/activate && pytest tests/parsing/test_canonical_line_roles.py -q`
- `. .venv/bin/activate && pytest tests/staging/test_import_session.py -q`

## Evidence

- Pre-fix local repro on the live policy classified `The Four Elements of Good Cooking` as `RECIPE_TITLE` and both `SALT` and `What is Salt?` as `INGREDIENT_LINE`.
- Post-fix regression test keeps the same atomized rows at `OTHER`.

## Constraints / Decision

Keep the fix narrow.
Outside-recipe structure still needs to support real stubs such as a recipe title followed by yield, quantity ingredients, or direct instruction, so the policy now requires actual local scaffold instead of any adjacent short heading.
