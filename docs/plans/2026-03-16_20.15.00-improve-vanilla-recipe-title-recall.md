# Improve Vanilla Recipe Title Recall

This ExecPlan is a living document. The sections `Progress`, `Surprises & Discoveries`, `Decision Log`, and `Outcomes & Retrospective` must be kept up to date as work proceeds.

This document must be maintained in accordance with `docs/PLANS.md`.

## Purpose / Big Picture

After this change, the deterministic line-role pass should recover more real recipe titles from the pulled gold books, especially titles that are long mixed-case headings, all-caps titles that begin with cooking verbs, and titles followed by a note line before ingredients. The user-visible proof is a better `per_label.RECIPE_TITLE` recall in canonical-text benchmark runs while the recent pseudo-recipe safeguards still hold on `saltfatacidheatCUTDOWN`.

Today the title detector is safe but too narrow. `data/golden/benchmark-vs-golden/2026-03-16_19.52.30-vanilla-title-sweep/vanilla_recipe_title_summary.md` shows micro title recall at `0.318`, with especially weak recall on `dinnerfor2cutdown`, `roastchickenandotherstoriescutdown`, and `thefoodlabcutdown`. The current misses are mostly real titles that deterministic rules downgrade before nearby recipe-start evidence can help them.

## Surprises & Discoveries

- Observation: outside-span title acceptance currently requires a compact all-caps heading, even when stronger recipe-start evidence is nearby.
  Evidence: `_outside_span_title_variant_allowed(...)` returns `False` unless `_looks_compact_heading(candidate.text)` is true, which blocks long recipe names like `Pan-Roasted Filets Mignons with Asparagus and Garlic-Herb Butter`.

- Observation: a title containing `with` can be deterministically labeled as `RECIPE_VARIANT` before the title detector ever evaluates it.
  Evidence: `_looks_variant_heading_text(...)` treats any line starting with `with ` as a variant and the deterministic path checks `variant_heading` before title context.

- Observation: all-caps titles that begin with a cooking verb can be captured as instructions.
  Evidence: `_looks_recipe_title(...)` and `_looks_variant_heading_text(...)` both hard-fail on `_INSTRUCTION_VERB_RE`, which makes `ROAST GROUSE WITH BREAD SAUCE AND GAME CRUMBS` unreachable as a title.

- Observation: some real recipe titles are followed by `NOTE:` prose before any ingredient line, and the current title-with-context helper rejects them.
  Evidence: `FOOLPROOF SOFT-BOILED EGGS` in `thefoodlabcutdown` is downgraded outside a span because `_looks_recipe_title_with_context(...)` only inspects the immediate next line and does not treat note-plus-recipe-start as valid context.

- Observation: the outside-span rescue rules cannot simply be reused inside confirmed recipe spans.
  Evidence: tightening outside-span title context initially broke `roastchickenandotherstoriescutdown` because legitimate in-span titles followed by immediate note prose were downgraded before ingredients appeared.

- Observation: Food Lab precision collapses quickly if TOC-style or generic technique headings are allowed to count as recipe-start support.
  Evidence: the first widened-context pass raised `thefoodlabcutdown` recall but also promoted headings like `THE BASIC PANTRY`, `POTS AND PANS`, and `How to Cut a Bell Pepper`.

## Outcomes & Retrospective

The deterministic pass now recovers three important title shapes that the earlier rules missed: long mixed-case recipe names that include connector words like `with`, all-caps titles that begin with cooking verbs, and titles separated from ingredients by a duplicate-title echo or `NOTE:` line. The implementation stayed local to `cookimport/parsing/canonical_line_roles.py` and added focused regressions in `tests/parsing/test_canonical_line_roles.py`.

The benchmark result is improved vanilla title recall on the worst books without reopening the pseudo-recipe overcount problem:

- `thefoodlabcutdown` moved from precision/recall/F1 `0.387 / 0.197 / 0.261` to `0.600 / 0.295 / 0.396` in `data/golden/benchmark-vs-golden/2026-03-16_20.03.31-title-recall-pass2/thefoodlabcutdown/`.
- `roastchickenandotherstoriescutdown` moved from `1.000 / 0.149 / 0.260` to `1.000 / 0.224 / 0.366` in `data/golden/benchmark-vs-golden/2026-03-16_20.18.00-roast-title-note-pass3/roastchickenandotherstoriescutdown/`.
- `dinnerfor2cutdown` improved from `1.000 / 0.167 / 0.286` to `1.000 / 0.200 / 0.333` in `data/golden/benchmark-vs-golden/2026-03-16_20.03.31-title-recall-pass2/dinnerfor2cutdown/`.
- `amatteroftastecutdown` moved from `0.842 / 0.525 / 0.647` to `0.826 / 0.623 / 0.710` in `data/golden/benchmark-vs-golden/2026-03-16_20.03.31-title-recall-pass2/amatteroftastecutdown/`.

The pass also exposed an unrelated staging issue: warning strings appended in place to the authoritative `ConversionReport` were dropped before serialization. `cookimport/staging/import_session.py` now appends warnings by reassignment so zero-recipe/fallback warnings survive report building.

## Context and Orientation

The deterministic line-role classifier lives in `cookimport/parsing/canonical_line_roles.py`. It takes `AtomicLineCandidate` rows from `cookimport/parsing/recipe_block_atomizer.py` and assigns labels such as `RECIPE_TITLE`, `RECIPE_VARIANT`, `INGREDIENT_LINE`, and `OTHER`. The most relevant helpers for this task are `_deterministic_label(...)`, `_sanitize_prediction(...)`, `_outside_span_title_variant_allowed(...)`, `_looks_recipe_title(...)`, `_looks_recipe_title_with_context(...)`, `_looks_subsection_heading_context(...)`, and `_looks_variant_heading_text(...)`.

The benchmark artifacts under `data/golden/benchmark-vs-golden/2026-03-16_19.52.30-vanilla-title-sweep/` are the current ground truth for this task. `vanilla_recipe_title_summary.md` gives the aggregate title precision and recall, while each book subdirectory contains `line-role-pipeline/line_role_predictions.jsonl` and aligned evaluation outputs that show the exact false negatives. The three primary miss patterns already reproduced from those artifacts are:

`dinnerfor2cutdown`: long mixed-case titles with `with` in the name, followed by a duplicate subtitle or yield/time lines.

`roastchickenandotherstoriescutdown`: all-caps titles that begin with a verb such as `ROAST`.

`thefoodlabcutdown`: title lines followed by `NOTE:` and then ingredient lines, which currently look too much like how-to section headings.

Tests for this logic live in `tests/parsing/test_canonical_line_roles.py`. The repository already has narrow deterministic tests there for outside-span title, variant, knowledge, and how-to behavior, so the new regressions should extend that file rather than creating a parallel test module.

## Plan of Work

First, loosen the title-shape heuristics without making them permissive in isolation. Update `_looks_recipe_title(...)` so a strong title-like heading can survive even when it starts with an instruction verb, as long as the line looks like a heading rather than prose. This should be paired with context-based gating, not a blanket allowance.

Next, narrow variant detection. `_looks_variant_heading_text(...)` should keep catching explicit variant headings like `FOR A CROWD`, but it should stop classifying long mixed-case recipe names as variants just because they contain or begin with `with`. If a line has full recipe-title shape and nearby yield/ingredient/instruction evidence, it should remain eligible for `RECIPE_TITLE`.

Then, strengthen `_looks_recipe_title_with_context(...)` and the outside-span sanitizer so note-led recipe starts are allowed. A title followed by `NOTE:` and then a yield line, ingredient line, or instruction-like line should count as recipe context. The same helper should also be used when deciding whether outside-span `RECIPE_TITLE` or `RECIPE_VARIANT` labels are allowed, so compact-heading shape is no longer the only path.

Finally, tighten the subsection-heading path so it does not steal obvious recipe titles. A compact all-caps title should become `HOWTO_SECTION` only when the local context genuinely looks like a subsection inside an existing recipe or technique explanation, not when the next one or two lines establish a new recipe body.

## Validation and Acceptance

Acceptance is behavioral. The new tests in `tests/parsing/test_canonical_line_roles.py` must prove that:

1. a long mixed-case title with `with` plus nearby yield/time evidence becomes `RECIPE_TITLE` outside a span;
2. an all-caps verb-led heading like `ROAST GROUSE WITH BREAD SAUCE AND GAME CRUMBS` becomes `RECIPE_TITLE`, not `INSTRUCTION_LINE`;
3. a title followed by `NOTE:` and then ingredients remains `RECIPE_TITLE`, not `HOWTO_SECTION` or `OTHER`.

The benchmark acceptance bar is not perfect recall. It is a clear improvement in `per_label.RECIPE_TITLE` recall on the worst books, with no obvious collapse in title precision and no regression of the recent `saltfatacidheatCUTDOWN` pseudo-recipe fix.

## Idempotence and Recovery

The test and benchmark commands are safe to rerun. If a heuristic broadening causes new false positives, back out only the specific title-shape or context change, keep the added regression tests, and re-run the worst-book benchmarks before widening the sweep again.
