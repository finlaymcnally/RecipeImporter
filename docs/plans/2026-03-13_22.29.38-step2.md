# Repair CodexFarm Staging and Label Ownership

This ExecPlan is a living document. The sections `Progress`, `Surprises & Discoveries`, `Decision Log`, and `Outcomes & Retrospective` must be kept up to date as work proceeds.

This document must be maintained in accordance with `docs/PLANS.md`.

This plan is one focused split of `docs/plans/2026-03-13_22.18.12-codexfarm-unified-reliability-plan.md`, but it is self-contained for a novice contributor.

## Purpose / Big Picture

After this change, CodexFarm should stop promoting obvious section headers, chapter prose, and off-span transitions into recipe titles, and it should stop letting Codex override cleaner deterministic or baseline labels for syntax-heavy classes without strong local evidence. The user-visible proof is that title and boundary regressions on the March 6 benchmark books become rarer without giving back the real title and instruction gains that made CodexFarm useful in the first place.

This plan is intentionally upstream-focused. It repairs the structure entering the recipe pipeline before touching deeper pass2 or pass3 logic.

## Surprises & Discoveries

- Observation: the March 13 report set does not merely say “add title guardrails.” It also says the line-role gains are asymmetric.
  Evidence: `docs/reports/codexfarm_fix_report.md` argues that `RECIPE_TITLE` and `INSTRUCTION_LINE` improved while `TIME_LINE` and `RECIPE_NOTES` got materially worse, which calls for explicit ownership rules rather than generic trust in Codex output.

- Observation: two deterministic staging paths can promote wrong titles even when the line-role output is noisy rather than catastrophic.
  Evidence: the March 13 title-fix plan calls out `cookimport/staging/stage_block_predictions.py` and `cookimport/staging/draft_v1.py` as concrete places where fallback promotion and weak projection rules can overwrite a good recipe name.

- Observation: title errors and boundary errors interact.
  Evidence: when an off-span header becomes a recipe title, later pipeline stages inherit a false recipe start and spend effort on the wrong window instead of merely labeling one line incorrectly.

## Decision Log

- Decision: make label ownership explicit in code and tests instead of leaving it as an informal preference.
  Rationale: the benchmark evidence is label-specific. If the policy stays implicit, later prompt or orchestrator changes can easily reintroduce the same `TIME_LINE` and `RECIPE_NOTES` regressions.
  Date/Author: 2026-03-13 / Codex

- Decision: keep deterministic or baseline-first ownership for syntax-heavy classes unless Codex has unusually strong contrary evidence.
  Rationale: labels such as `TIME_LINE`, `YIELD_LINE`, section-header markers, strong `RECIPE_NOTES` patterns, and high-confidence `INGREDIENT_LINE` cases are mostly format-driven. Codex should help only when local evidence is truly stronger than the deterministic read.
  Date/Author: 2026-03-13 / Codex

- Decision: do not try to redesign the full line-role pipeline in this plan.
  Rationale: the goal is to stop the measured regressions with small, testable upstream rules. A full line-role redesign would blur the benchmark attribution and create too much overlap with other work.
  Date/Author: 2026-03-13 / Codex

## Context and Orientation

The key files for this plan are `cookimport/staging/stage_block_predictions.py` and `cookimport/staging/draft_v1.py`.

In this repository, staging means the deterministic layer that turns recipe candidates plus line-role evidence into an initial structured draft. `stage_block_predictions.py` decides which candidate block looks like a title or a recipe-local structural line. `draft_v1.py` projects staged spans back onto recipe objects and can overwrite `recipe.name` based on projected title spans.

Label ownership means deciding, for each class of line-role label, whether deterministic logic or Codex output gets the first claim. Baseline-first or deterministic-first means the existing deterministic or vanilla interpretation stays in force unless Codex has a strong reason to override it. For this plan, syntax-heavy labels are labels that are mostly driven by formatting and local lexical structure rather than broad semantic context. The important ones are `TIME_LINE`, `YIELD_LINE`, strong `RECIPE_NOTES` patterns, section headers, and high-confidence `INGREDIENT_LINE` cases.

The benchmark books that matter most here are `thefoodlabcutdown` and `seaandsmokecutdown`, because the March 13 reports cite concrete title-promotion failures there. `saltfatacidheatcutdown` still matters as part of the shared benchmark slice, but it is not the highest-yield validation book for this specific upstream plan.

## Plan of Work

Start in `cookimport/staging/stage_block_predictions.py`. Add an explicit title-candidate validity check that requires recipe-local boundary evidence before promoting `RECIPE_TITLE` or `RECIPE_VARIANT`. Boundary evidence can include nearby ingredient runs, time or yield lines that belong to the same recipe, or instruction-like lines immediately following the candidate. The title gate must reject lines that sit inside obvious prose runs, experiment or essay framing, or off-span transitions with no downstream recipe structure.

Then edit `cookimport/staging/draft_v1.py`. When projected title spans are applied back onto draft recipes, preserve an already-credible recipe name unless the new title candidate passes the same boundary-aware guardrails. This prevents section headers or stray spans from overwriting a better title late in the deterministic pipeline.

After title handling is stable, implement explicit label ownership. Keep deterministic or baseline-first authority for `TIME_LINE`, `YIELD_LINE`, strong `RECIPE_NOTES` patterns, section-header markers, and high-confidence `INGREDIENT_LINE` cases unless Codex has unusually strong contrary evidence. Record disagreement reasons when a Codex output is accepted over the deterministic read or when it is vetoed. The rule set does not need to be generic. It should encode the specific asymmetries the March 13 reports already measured.

Update the relevant staging tests in `tests/staging/test_stage_block_predictions.py`, `tests/staging/test_draft_v1_priority6.py`, and `tests/staging/test_draft_v1_staging_alignment.py`. Add fixtures for known false-title cases from The Food Lab and Sea and Smoke, plus positive controls where a real recipe title still promotes correctly.

Keep docs updates short. If new disagreement or ownership fields become benchmark-visible, document them briefly in `docs/07-bench/07-bench_README.md` or the nearest relevant staging note. Do not add broad design commentary to `AGENTS.md`.

## Validation and Acceptance

Acceptance is behavior-first.

First, the focused staging tests must prove that false-title cases no longer promote, positive-control recipe titles still promote, and strong deterministic `TIME_LINE` or `RECIPE_NOTES` cases do not get overridden without strong local evidence.

Second, benchmark reruns must show fewer obvious title/header promotions and no broad collapse of `TIME_LINE`, `YIELD_LINE`, or strong `RECIPE_NOTES` behavior. The point is not perfect title detection; it is that the specific March 13 failure family becomes materially less common.

Third, disagreement handling must be explainable. If Codex wins over the deterministic read, the local evidence should be visible in code and, where feasible, in diagnostics. If Codex is vetoed, that veto should also be explainable.

## Idempotence and Recovery

This plan is additive and safe to rerun. The tests and benchmark commands can be repeated while keeping fresh timestamped output roots. If a new ownership rule is too broad and suppresses real title gains, narrow the veto to the measured bad contexts rather than deleting ownership boundaries entirely. Do not overwrite the March 6 baseline root.
