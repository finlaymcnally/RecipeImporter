The 2026-03-17_20.27.19 benchmark confirms the new intended architecture landed: line-role shard prompts say no prior recipe-span authority is provided, and pre-grouping shard inputs carry `within_recipe_span: null`.

After that architectural fix, the remaining accuracy gap is mostly a knowledge/non-recipe classification problem rather than a recipe-span gating problem.

Key patterns from `saltfatacidheatcutdown/codexfarm`:

- Outside-recipe is still the dominant hard slice: `line-role-pipeline/slice_metrics.json` reports `1168` outside-recipe lines at only `0.5830` overall accuracy.
- Gold `KNOWLEDGE` is most often missed as `OTHER`. From `wrong_label_lines.jsonl` + `missed_gold_lines.jsonl`, the top miss path is `KNOWLEDGE -> OTHER` (`356` rows). Examples include `HOW SALT WORKS`, `How Salt Affects . . .`, `Meat`, and long explanatory prose paragraphs about salting/brining.
- There is a secondary over-promotion problem where non-recipe chapter/section headings are labeled as recipe structure. Examples: `SALT`, `What is Salt?`, `How Salt Works`, `Kitchen Basics`, `About the Author` becoming `HOWTO_SECTION`, and `The Four Elements of Good Cooking` becoming `RECIPE_TITLE`.
- `joined_line_table.jsonl` shows the strongest success path is outside-recipe explanatory prose that Codex upgrades to `KNOWLEDGE` (`332` rows). So Codex is helping a lot, but it still leaves a large tail of knowledge headings + short concept labels in `OTHER`.
- `knowledge_budget.json` shows all `KNOWLEDGE` predictions remain outside recipe (`663` total, `0` inside), so the issue is not knowledge leaking into accepted recipe spans.

Practical takeaway:

- The next leverage is not more span work.
- It is better handling for non-recipe pedagogical structure:
  - cookbook concept headings (`HOW SALT WORKS`, `Meat`, `Seafood`, `Fat`, etc.)
  - explanatory prose that lacks strong explicit knowledge cue phrases
  - guarding recipe-ish structural labels (`HOWTO_SECTION`, `RECIPE_TITLE`) so book/chapter headings do not absorb non-recipe TOC/section material.
