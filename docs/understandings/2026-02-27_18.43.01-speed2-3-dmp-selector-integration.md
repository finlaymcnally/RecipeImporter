## Discovery

- Current canonical-text alignment/runtime telemetry already centers on `COOKIMPORT_BENCHMARK_SEQUENCE_MATCHER` (`auto|stdlib|cydifflib|cdifflib`), so adding `dmp` there is lower-risk than introducing a second backend env contract.
- `fast-diff-match-patch` can be wrapped in a SequenceMatcher-compatible adapter quickly (`get_matching_blocks`, `get_opcodes`, `ratio`) and exposed as `COOKIMPORT_BENCHMARK_SEQUENCE_MATCHER=dmp`.
- dmp does not produce stdlib-identical opcode/matching-block boundaries, so it should not be treated as a strict drop-in parity backend.

## Evidence

- Bench script results after integration:
  - `python scripts/bench_sequence_matcher_impl.py --tokens 800 --repeats 2 --modes stdlib,dmp,auto`
    - stdlib mean `1.137674s`
    - dmp mean `0.000668s` (~1703x faster)
    - auto (`cydifflib`) mean `0.095896s` (~11.9x faster than stdlib)
  - `python scripts/bench_sequence_matcher_impl.py --tokens 1800 --repeats 3 --modes stdlib,dmp,auto`
    - stdlib mean `11.754282s`
    - dmp mean `0.001753s` (~6707x faster)
    - auto (`cydifflib`) mean `1.292526s` (~9.1x faster than stdlib)
- Regression check:
  - `pytest -q tests/bench/test_sequence_matcher_dropin_parity.py -k dmp`
  - canonical minimal fixture scoring remains equal between forced `stdlib` and forced `dmp`.
- Synthetic canonical evaluator run (`evaluate_canonical_text`, legacy strategy, mismatch-heavy text):
  - stdlib: `total_seconds=3.071774`, `alignment_sequence_matcher_seconds=3.067203`
  - dmp: `total_seconds=0.008898`, `alignment_sequence_matcher_seconds=0.001028`
