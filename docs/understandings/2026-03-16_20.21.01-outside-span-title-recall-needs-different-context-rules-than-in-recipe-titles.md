# Outside-span title recall needs different context rules than in-recipe title retention

- Outside recipe spans and inside recipe spans cannot share the exact same title-context rule.
- Outside spans need stricter support because TOC rows, knowledge headings, and `How to ...` technique headings can look title-like and sit next to ingredient-like or action-heavy prose.
- Inside an already accepted recipe span, immediate note prose is enough to keep a title alive even when no yield/ingredient line appears immediately after it.

Evidence from the 2026-03-16 recall pass:

- `thefoodlabcutdown` pass 1 improved title recall from `0.197` to `0.328`, but precision collapsed from `0.387` to `0.278` because headings like `THE BASIC PANTRY`, `POTS AND PANS`, and `How to Cut a Bell Pepper` were upgraded to `RECIPE_TITLE`.
- Tightening outside-span support by rejecting TOC-like ingredient followers, ignoring generic action-heavy prose as title support, and hard-blocking `How to ...` headings recovered `thefoodlabcutdown` to `precision=0.600`, `recall=0.295`, `f1=0.396`.
- That outside-span trim initially hurt `roastchickenandotherstoriescutdown` because some true titles are inside an already grouped recipe and are followed by note prose instead of immediate ingredient/yield structure.
- Restoring a separate in-recipe allowance for immediate note prose lifted `roastchickenandotherstoriescutdown` from the original `recall=0.149` to `0.224` while keeping `precision=1.000`.

Practical rule split:

- Outside recipe spans:
  - allow title rescue for long mixed-case titles, verb-led all-caps titles, and note-led starts;
  - skip duplicate-title and explicit note lines when scanning context;
  - do not treat TOC-like numeric headings, `How to ...` technique headings, or generic narrative/action prose as recipe-start support.
- Inside recipe spans:
  - keep the looser title retention path;
  - immediate recipe-note prose is sufficient evidence that the title belongs to the already accepted recipe flow.
