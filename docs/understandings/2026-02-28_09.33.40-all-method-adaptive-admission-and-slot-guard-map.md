# All-Method Adaptive Admission And Slot Guard Map

The all-method scheduler has three interacting control layers now: split-slot capping (`split_phase_slot_mode` and slot caps), per-config split-worker capping (`split_worker_cap_*`), and adaptive admission decisions (`admission_active_cap`, `admission_guard_target`, `admission_reason`).

Resource guard capping is resolved once in `_resolve_all_method_scheduler_runtime(...)` and then applied consistently in both `_run_all_method_benchmark(...)` and `_run_all_method_benchmark_global_queue(...)`.

Adaptive admission no longer blindly over-admits before eval tail. It keeps configured inflight as the pre-eval ceiling, then uses dynamic guard targets and telemetry-driven clamp/boost decisions while preserving `max_active_during_eval` constraints.

New scheduler telemetry fields in `scheduler_timeseries.jsonl` and scheduler summaries are the source of truth for why queue refill changed over time (`adaptive_admission_*`, split-slot cap fields, and CPU high-water).
