## Discovery

The cleanest way to finish speed1-4 without metric drift is to keep `evaluate_stage_blocks(...)` and `evaluate_canonical_text(...)` untouched, and move overlap into a streaming replay boundary.

During `execution_mode=pipelined`, prediction now emits per-block `PredictionRecord`s into a bounded queue while a consumer thread validates and ingests those records in real time. At end-of-stream, the consumer materializes replay artifacts from the consumed stream and the normal evaluator runs on those finalized paths.

This gives true predict/consume overlap and keeps report semantics stable, because scoring logic remains path-based and unchanged.

## Implementation Surfaces

- `cookimport/cli.py`
  - `_build_prediction_bundle_from_stage_records(..., require_contiguous=...)`
  - `run_pipelined(...)`
  - `labelstudio_benchmark(...)`

## Constraint Learned

Zero-block prediction runs are valid in canonical fixtures. Streaming replay reconstruction must therefore allow an empty stream and fall back to original stage paths instead of failing on "no records".
