# Unified Reliability Plan Coverage

The unified plan captures the main consensus well: upstream title/boundary guardrails, pass1/pass2 transport and overlap repair, stricter structural-success rules, pass3 routing discipline, non-agentic structured execution, and benchmark-gated evaluation of a merged pass2/pass3 prototype.

The main thing it flattens is one report's stronger line-role arbitration story. `docs/reports/codexfarm_fix_report.md` argues not just for title guardrails, but for explicit label-aware ownership and arbitration where deterministic or vanilla logic keeps labels like `TIME_LINE` and `RECIPE_NOTES` unless Codex has unusually strong contrary evidence. The unified plan keeps the structural parts of that idea but does not foreground the label-policy aspect.

It also softens a few sharper recommendations from the reports: disagreement-driven review instead of confidence gating, monotonic fallback as a design rule, and explicit deterministic ownership of crisp syntax-heavy labels. Those ideas are compatible with the unified plan, but they are not as visible as the transport/merged-stage themes.
