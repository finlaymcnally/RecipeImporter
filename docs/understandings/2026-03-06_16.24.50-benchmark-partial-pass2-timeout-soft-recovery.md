summary: "Benchmark-mode codex-farm runs now keep high-coverage partial pass2 output even when timeout rows are mixed with content-filter/no-last-agent-message failures."
read_when:
  - When debugging why a benchmark codexfarm book continued despite `failure_categories` including `timeout`
  - When changing benchmark-mode partial-output recovery thresholds in codex_farm_runner
---

- The orchestrator already knows how to handle missing pass2 bundles recipe-by-recipe:
  - missing outputs become `pass2_error`,
  - surviving recipes continue to pass3/eval,
  - comparison naturally excludes only the missing recipes.
- The old blocker was `SubprocessCodexFarmRunner`: any mixed `timeout` bucket forced a hard failure before the orchestrator could drop missing recipes.
- Current policy:
  - always recover on `no last agent message` / `nonzero_exit_no_payload` only,
  - in benchmark mode, also recover when failure categories stay within `nonzero_exit_no_payload` + `timeout` and output coverage is still high:
    - at least 80% of expected `.json` bundles were written,
    - at most 3 bundles are missing.
- Recovery messaging now includes the bundle counts (`written/expected`, missing count) so benchmark logs make the partial comparison explicit.
- Non-benchmark recipe runs still fail hard on mixed timeout partial exits.
