# Structure Gap Is Mostly Recipe-Span Gating, Not LLM Reading Failure

For `data/golden/benchmark-vs-golden/2026-03-17_16.06.55/single-offline-benchmark/saltfatacidheatcutdown`, the main structure misses are not best explained by the LLM failing to understand recipe prose.

Key evidence from `codexfarm/line-role-pipeline/joined_line_table.jsonl`:

- `INSTRUCTION_LINE`: 63 gold rows total, 47 wrong, and all 47 wrong rows are already outside recipe spans (`within_recipe_span=false|null`).
- `INGREDIENT_LINE`: 170 gold rows total, 49 wrong, and 48 of those 49 wrong rows are already outside recipe spans.
- Example false-outside recipe lines include obvious instructions such as:
  - `Quarter the cabbage through the core...`
  - `In a small bowl, toss the sliced onion with the lemon juice...`
  - `Preheat the oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet with paper towels.`
- Example false-outside recipe ingredient runs include:
  - `1 large jalapeño, seeds and veins removed if desired, thinly sliced`
  - `3 scallions, finely sliced`
  - `1 /4 cup neutral-tasting oil`

Implication:

- The current benchmark architecture is not giving the LLM a clean “read this cookbook and label the recipe structure” job.
- Instead, accepted recipe spans gate where structured labels are safe, and once real recipe content falls outside those spans, the final authority path tends to turn it into `KNOWLEDGE`, `OTHER`, or fallback noise.
- Prompt/model upgrades alone are unlikely to move this run near 90-100 percent until recipe-span acceptance/retention and outside-span structured-label policy improve.
