## Discovery

`eval_report.json` is not a reliable byte-parity target for stdlib-vs-auto matcher checks because telemetry fields (implementation name and timing subphases) are expected to differ even when alignment decisions are identical.

`aligned_prediction_blocks.jsonl` is the right comparison surface because:
- it is deterministic (stable row order + sorted keys),
- it captures the concrete per-block mapping decisions from canonical alignment,
- and it can be compared byte-for-byte directly between stdlib and accelerated runs.

## Practical implication

For strict parity checks, diff `aligned_prediction_blocks.jsonl` (and optionally check high-level score equality). Use `eval_report.json` for telemetry/perf interpretation, not raw byte-identical parity.
