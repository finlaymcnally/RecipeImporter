# Confidence / trust runtime seams

- The only live control-path use of mixed line-role confidence was the Codex escalation gate in `cookimport/parsing/canonical_line_roles.py`; grouping and Stage 7 ownership were already label-driven.
- The authoritative seam is wider on the write side than the decision side: once line metadata changes, the same fields need to flow through `label_source_of_truth.py`, `staging/import_session.py`, `labelstudio/ingest.py`, follow-up exports, and `scripts/benchmark_cutdown_for_external_ai.py`.
- Reviewer tooling was teaching the old model in two separate places:
  - `cookimport/bench/followup_bundle.py` and `cookimport/cf_debug_cli.py` exposed uncertainty as thresholded confidence.
  - `scripts/benchmark_cutdown_for_external_ai.py` surfaced `analysis.low_confidence_changed_lines_packet` and `analysis.line_role_confidence` in the upload bundle.
- Practical migration rule: keep `confidence` as a derived alias of `trust_score` on the label-first seam so old readers still work, but move new reasoning and packet names to `trust_score`, `escalation_score`, and `escalation_reasons`.
