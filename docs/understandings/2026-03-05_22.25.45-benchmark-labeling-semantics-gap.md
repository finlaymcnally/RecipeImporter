---
summary: "Current analytics benchmark labeling conflates official paired variant names with actual AI-assistance state."
read_when:
  - "When fixing dashboard or compare/control benchmark labels"
  - "When deciding whether a row may safely be shown as vanilla or AI off"
---

- `cookimport/analytics/dashboard_render.py` and `cookimport/analytics/compare_control_engine.py` both currently infer `vanilla` from either a `/vanilla/` path segment or `llm_recipe_pipeline=off`, which is too weak because it ignores `line_role_pipeline`.
- `aiEffortLabelForRecord(...)` in both implementations then turns inferred `vanilla` into `AI off`, so a hybrid row can be mislabeled twice: once as the wrong variant and again as the wrong AI state.
- Official single-offline `vanilla` is still a valid concept because `_interactive_single_offline_variants(...)` in `cookimport/cli.py` explicitly forces `llm_recipe_pipeline=off`, `line_role_pipeline=off`, and `atomic_block_splitter=off` for that authored variant.
- The fix should preserve official paired `vanilla` / `codexfarm` labeling while adding a separate semantic classifier for actual assistance state, at minimum distinguishing deterministic, line-role-only, recipe-only, full-stack, and unknown rows.
