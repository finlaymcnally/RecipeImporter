# Compact Knowledge Payload Review

Fresh zero-token previews on `thefoodlabcutdown` and `saltfatacidheatcutdown` show that the compact alias-based payload still carries the important model-facing evidence:

- chunk-local block text is still present
- heading/table cues survive where available
- optional context/guardrails are omitted cleanly when empty

The remaining quality risk is upstream hint bias, not transport density.

Representative compact chunks still mark obvious non-knowledge material with `h.l="knowledge"` and `h.s="instructional"`, including:

- Salt Fat praise blurbs
- Simon & Schuster signup / marketing copy
- section and recipe index pages such as `Recipes and Recommendations`
- Food Lab front matter such as `PREFACE`

So the compacting appears quality-safe by itself, but future knowledge-stage improvements should focus on fixing `suggested_lane` / `semantic_hint` quality rather than restoring the old verbose payload.
