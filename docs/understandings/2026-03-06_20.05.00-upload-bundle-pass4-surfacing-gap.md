The upload-bundle gap was not that pass4 artifacts were missing from successful per-book benchmark roots; the main problem was surfacing. Full per-book bundles already carried files like `prompts/prompt_task4_pass4_knowledge.txt`, `prompts/prompt_type_samples_from_full_prompt_log.md`, and `prediction-run/raw/llm/<slug>/pass4_knowledge_manifest.json`, but the multi-book high-level `upload_bundle_v1` selected only `prompt_budget_summary.json` and omitted every pass4-specific prompt artifact, so an external reviewer could miss the entire knowledge-harvest pass.

For the 2026-03-06_15.22.11 run, `02_saltfatacidheatcutdown`, `03_seaandsmokecutdown`, and `04_thefoodlabcutdown` had real pass4 artifacts under `codexfarm/`, while `01_dinnerfor2cutdown/codexfarm/` was empty. The fix therefore needs two layers: keep lightweight pass4 evidence in high-level group bundles, and make both group and per-book bundle indexes explicitly advertise pass4 via summary fields and row locators instead of assuming a reviewer will discover the right payload rows unaided.
