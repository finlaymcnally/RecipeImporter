# Legacy Purge Tail

The last easy burn-the-boats deletions were not in the core runtime. They were in the command/test surfaces and the checked-in recipe pack assets.

- `cookimport/cli.py` still exposed hidden pass1/pass2/pass3 and selective-retry knobs even after the single-correction runtime cutover. Removing those parameters was safe once the remaining callers/tests stopped passing them.
- Prompt-artifact helpers were already compatible with the current manifest shape, but the benchmark helper fixtures and prompt-artifact tests were still constructing `chunking/schemaorg/final` trees by hand. Converting those fixtures to `recipe_correction`, `knowledge`, and `tags` brought the tests back in line with the live contract.
- The old recipe pack files under `llm_pipelines/` were not referenced by live code after the transport tests moved to `recipe.correction.compact.v1`, `recipe.knowledge.compact.v1`, and `recipe.tags.v1`. That made them safe to delete outright.
- The biggest remaining non-historical legacy seam is `scripts/benchmark_cutdown_for_external_ai.py` and its paired test. That path still models recipe work through pass1/pass2/pass3 internals and needs its own focused sweep if the repo is meant to be completely free of the old benchmark topology.
