---
summary: "CodexFarm structural invariants are easiest to keep stable when transport/runtime checks feed shared contract-level audits instead of ad hoc orchestrator strings."
read_when:
  - When changing CodexFarm structural success/failure rules
  - When a test fixture starts failing because pass2 output no longer matches the transported source span
---

- Pass1->pass2 transport already had the right observable surface (`transport_audit/*.json`), but it only exposed mismatch booleans and legacy mismatch labels. The durable fix was to add a nested `verification` payload with stable reason codes plus row counts (`source_row_count`, `effective_row_count`, `payload_row_count`) and `source_hash`.
- Structural success needed one shared definition point. Moving pass2/pass3 checks into `cookimport/llm/codex_farm_contracts.py` made the orchestrator consume one result shape (`status`, `severity`, `reason_codes`) instead of inventing new error text at each call site.
- Several older orchestrator tests were implicitly relying on unrealistic pass2 fixtures: extracted ingredients/instructions that did not actually appear in the transported evidence span. Once `extractive_text_not_in_transport_span` became a hard reason, those fixtures had to be made source-grounded or they correctly stopped reaching pass3.
