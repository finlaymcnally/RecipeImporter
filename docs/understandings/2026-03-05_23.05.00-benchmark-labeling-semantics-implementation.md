---
summary: "Analytics benchmark labeling needs two layers: official paired variant identity and actual AI-assistance profile."
read_when:
  - "When changing dashboard or compare/control benchmark labels"
  - "When deciding whether a row may be shown as vanilla or AI off"
---

- The bug came from treating `llm_recipe_pipeline=off` as enough to infer `vanilla`, which also made `AI off` wrong for hybrid rows with `line_role_pipeline` still enabled.
- The stable fix is to classify records twice:
  - `benchmark_variant`: preserve `vanilla` / `codexfarm` only for official paired `single-offline-benchmark` and `single-profile-benchmark` paths whose runtime settings actually match deterministic/full-stack expectations.
  - `ai_assistance_profile`: classify actual runtime behavior as `deterministic`, `line_role_only`, `recipe_only`, `full_stack`, or `other`.
- Dashboard quick-filter semantics remain stricter than variant semantics: `Official benchmarks only` still targets official single-offline paired rows, while paired single-profile rows still keep `vanilla` / `codexfarm` variant identity for diagnostics and trend pairing.
