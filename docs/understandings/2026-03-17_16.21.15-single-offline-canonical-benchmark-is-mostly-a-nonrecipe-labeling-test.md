# Single-Offline Canonical Benchmark Is Mostly A Non-Recipe Labeling Test

For `data/golden/benchmark-vs-golden/2026-03-17_16.06.55/single-offline-benchmark/saltfatacidheatcutdown`, the main remaining error source is not boundary/segmentation failure.

- `codexfarm/eval_report.json` reports boundary `{correct: 1197, over: 0, under: 0, partial: 0}` and alignment coverage above 99 percent.
- `codexfarm/wrong_label_lines.jsonl` is dominated by `KNOWLEDGE->OTHER` (185 rows) and `OTHER->KNOWLEDGE` (161 rows).
- Those two confusion directions alone account for about two thirds of codexfarm wrong-label rows (345 of 517).
- Vanilla has the same basic failure mode (`KNOWLEDGE->OTHER` 306, `OTHER->KNOWLEDGE` 97), so the codex uplift mainly comes from recovering knowledge rows while also over-promoting some lines into knowledge.
- Additional weaker seams are `INSTRUCTION_LINE->KNOWLEDGE` (39 rows), `INGREDIENT_LINE->OTHER` (33), and very poor `RECIPE_VARIANT` / `RECIPE_NOTES` recall.

Implication: this benchmark behaves much more like a broad canonical line-labeling test, including difficult non-recipe semantics, than a pure recipe-structure test. Near-100-percent accuracy is not a realistic expectation unless the target scope is narrowed or the non-recipe authority improves dramatically.
